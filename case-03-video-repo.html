▍ SECTION 01 THE PROBLEM
01
Diagnosis

The marketing team requested a significant video production budget because "reps aren't using video." Management was prepared to fund it. But the real issue wasn't content scarcity, it was content chaos.

What was actually happening

  • Hundreds of videos and PDFs already existed in the repository.
  • Search returned irrelevant results. "Demo" surfaced training videos, podcasts, and old unrelated content.
  • Navigation required tribal knowledge. Product line folders → legacy categories → file names. New reps were lost.
  • Metadata was inconsistent, so new content was effectively invisible.
  • Reps didn't know what existed, so they asked the org to make more, instead of looking.
▍ SECTION 02 DISCOVERY & DIAGNOSIS
02
Research

User research

  • Conducted 15 interviews with reps across regions and experience levels.
  • Shadowed 8 sales calls to understand when and how reps needed video.
  • Analyzed search logs: 34% of searches returned zero results.
  • Reviewed repository content: found videos on the exact topics reps were requesting, they were just undiscoverable.
Problem is discoverability, not production volume.

Reps needed videos for key moments: intro calls, objection handling, product walkthroughs, and closing. But the repository was organized by product, not by use case. A rep doing a product walkthrough would have to know it existed and know where to look.

▍ SECTION 03 APPROACH
03
Build

Reframing the investment

I argued: we are already spending on production of new videos, they were simply not being watched. Instead, we should better organize and improve discovery of what exists. If that doesn't work, then we invest in production. Leadership agreed to a 60-day pilot.

Engineering & UX collaboration

Collaborated with engineering and UX to define product requirements, worked through technical constraints and trade-offs, and iterated on design based on user feedback. When Engineering initially pushed for legacy infrastructure, I made the case for a new tagging system based on user research data, and we shipped it.

The redesign

  • New tagging system. Every video tagged by use case, product, and seniority. Reps could find "discovery call videos for new SDRs."
  • Improved search. AI-powered search that understood sales intent, not just keywords.
  • Metadata cleanup. Standardized titles, descriptions, and metadata across hundreds of assets.
  • Curated playlists for common sales moments, first call, objection handling, demo.
  • New UI. Replaced the file-browser experience with a card-based grid discoverable by use case.
  • Rating system. Reps rate videos so the most useful content floats to the top.

Execution roadmap

  1. Data audit, tagging system design, and quick-win tagging on top 300 videos.
  2. Full repository tagging across all hundreds of assets.
  3. UI redesign and search improvement.
  4. Launch, monitoring, iterate on taxonomy based on search behavior.
▍ SECTION 04 RESULTS
04
Receipts

Drove a 691% increase in video views within one month of launch. The platform became the primary video delivery system for 3,000+ sales reps across Meta's global sales org.

Engagement

  • 691% increase in video views in Q1 post-launch (vs. Q4 baseline).
  • 57% increase in PDF downloads.
  • Search results with zero hits dropped from 34% to 4%.
  • Top-10 most-watched videos had 0 overlap with the pre-launch top 10. The new taxonomy was surfacing previously hidden content.

Business impact

  • Production budget saved. The $150K request was rejected. The $20K investment in discovery returned ROI.
  • Content ROI improved. Existing content that cost $0 in Q4 now drives engagement.
  • Ramp time. New reps found onboarding videos 84% faster, improving time-to-first-call.
  • Precedent. Changed how the org thinks about content strategy — discovery before production.
▍ SECTION 05 KEY LEARNINGS
01 / LEARNING
Organization beats production.

An organized library of old content beats a disorganized library of new content. Reps use what they can find.

02 / LEARNING
Listen to the problem, not the ask.

"We need more video" was the ask. "I can't find what I need" was the problem. Those aren't the same thing.

03 / LEARNING
Metadata matters.

How you tag and categorize content determines whether it gets used. Spend time on taxonomy.

04 / LEARNING
Measure what indicates real value.

Vanity: "We made more videos." Real: "Reps found what they needed faster — and it showed up in sales performance."
