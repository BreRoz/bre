▍ SECTION 01 THE PROBLEM
01
Diagnosis

Sales reps were spending 8+ hours per week searching for materials across multiple systems. Knowledge existed, but findability was broken. Teams were creating unapproved content out of frustration which created compliance risk. Platform adoption was low despite significant investment in content creation.

Root causes

  • Search functionality was inadequate. The platform's search didn't understand sales context. Searching for "objection handling" returned irrelevant results.
  • Navigation required domain expertise. Reps had to know the taxonomy to find anything. New reps were lost.
  • Content discoverability was passive. Without active search, reps missed relevant resources.
  • No integration with sales workflow. The platform felt disconnected from where reps actually worked, the CRM.
▍ SECTION 02 APPROACH
02
Reframe

Rather than add more content, I reframed the problem as a user workflow and productivity challenge. This shifted the conversation from "we need more materials" to "we need to make materials findable and actionable."

Discovery phase

  • Conducted multiple round table interviews with reps across regions, products, and tenure levels.
  • Shadowed sales calls to understand when reps needed information to be quickly discoverable.
  • Analyzed search logs and platform telemetry to identify gaps.
  • Built personas for reps at different career stages.
Search, not content, was the adoption blocker.

Strategic positioning

Reframed to leadership as a dual-value play: sales effectiveness (better close rates, faster ramp) + productivity gains (fewer hours spent searching). This secured buy-in from sales leadership and C-level.

Design & execution

  • Defined product roadmap and translated business requirements into technical specifications for the development team.
  • New search experience. Implemented a semantic search that understood sales intent, not just keywords.
  • Smart navigation. Layered by use case + product line + sales stage.
  • Content organization. Moved from rigid taxonomy to dynamic tagging. Content lives in multiple contexts.
  • Onboarding redesign. Short, workflow-based training instead of long content library tours.
▍ SECTION 03 RESULTS
03
Receipts

Adoption & engagement

  • 71% increase in platform traffic quarter-over-quarter post-launch.
  • 53% increase in material downloads from the platform.
  • Search now accounted for 67% of traffic — up from 12%.

Business impact

  • Sales productivity. Reps saved 4 hours per week on average through faster material discovery.
  • Sales effectiveness. 2.75% lift in close rate for power users (heavy platform usage).
▍ SECTION 04 KEY LEARNINGS
01 / LEARNING
Search is a feature, not a nice-to-have.

When findability is broken, users work around the system. Good search is the foundation of any knowledge platform.

02 / LEARNING
Reframe problems as user problems.

"We need more content" is a feature request. "Reps need to find information faster during calls" is a user problem with many possible solutions.

03 / LEARNING
Integrate with existing workflows.

A great tool that requires new habits won't succeed. Meet users where they already are.

04 / LEARNING
Measure what matters.

Usage metrics are vanity. Measure what actually impacts sales: call prep time, close rate, ramp speed, and compliance.
